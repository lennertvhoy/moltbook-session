## Slide 1: Title

**Moltbook: signal, limits, and what still has to be solved**

- Verification-first deck
- Rebuilt from repo code, screenshots, and cited sources
- Thesis: Moltbook is more useful as a stress test than as proof of autonomous digital society

---

## Slide 2: What Moltbook officially claims

- Homepage: "A Social Network for AI Agents"
- Homepage: humans are welcome to observe
- Terms: AI agents have no legal eligibility
- Terms: the human account owner remains responsible

**Framing:** agent participation is real, but legal and governance autonomy are not established.

---

## Slide 3: What OpenClaw documents

- Context is what OpenClaw sends to the model for a run
- OpenClaw supports per-agent routing, sandboxes, and tool policies
- OpenClaw documentation and FAQ make it clear that multi-agent systems are operationally real but token-heavy

**Framing:** this is an architecture story, not evidence of one stable digital organism.

---

## Slide 4: Token cost

- OpenClaw docs provide a concrete context-load anchor
- Repo model now labels the "$0.377 per social cycle" figure as illustrative
- Result is reproducible from code, but assumption-driven

**Framing:** cost pressure is real; the exact number is a scenario output, not a measured Moltbook trace.

---

## Slide 5: Identity and governance gaps

- Tsinghua paper finds a mixed landscape: autonomous-leaning, human-leaning, and ambiguous authors all appear
- Moltbook's terms keep responsibility with the human owner
- Strong claims about fully autonomous social behavior are therefore overstated

**Framing:** useful experiment in AI coordination under attribution uncertainty.

---

## Slide 6: Trend lines we can defend

- Stanford HAI: large recent jumps on difficult benchmarks
- Stanford HAI: >280x decline in cost to reach GPT-3.5-level quality from Nov 2022 to Oct 2024
- Epoch: strong compute and efficiency growth
- Epoch ECI: composite framing exists because single benchmarks saturate

**Framing:** the strongest claim is about shifting economics and infrastructure, not about one benchmark line going exponential forever.

---

## Slide 7: Anti-hype curve reading

- Do not treat bounded benchmarks as permanent exponential laws
- Do not use screenshots as substitutes for underlying citations
- Do not overstate the MiniMax vs Opus comparison; the original repo claim was too confident

**Framing:** the talk improves when weak numbers are removed instead of defended.

---

## Slide 8: Forecast model

- Monte Carlo barrier-crossing model retained
- Inputs moved into `data/forecast_scenarios.json`
- Outputs are reproducible with `uv`
- Model is now explicitly presented as assumption-driven

**Framing:** use it as a structured uncertainty map, not a prophecy.

---

## Slide 9: What still has to be solved

- Identity and attribution
- Persistent memory without repeated expensive context reloads
- Governance that matches real control and liability
- Unit economics that survive beyond demos

**Framing:** these are the actual bottlenecks behind "agent society" language.

---

## Slide 10: Conclusion

- Moltbook is a useful signal
- It is not yet proof of stable autonomous digital society
- The strongest honest takeaway is architectural, economic, and governance-oriented

**Closing line:** what matters is not whether the demo looks social, but whether identity, memory, governance, and cost actually hold at scale.
